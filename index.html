Frequently Asked Questions

What does an Enrolled Agent (EA) do?

EAs are federally licensed tax practitioners authorized to represent taxpayers before the IRS in audits, collections, and appeals. I handle tax preparation and planning, respond to IRS notices, and represent clients through resolution.

How should I prepare for an IRS audit?

Gather source documents (bank statements, receipts, invoices, payroll filings, and prior returns), ensure books are reconciled, and organize support by year and category. I can help assemble, review, and present documentation and communicate with the IRS on your behalf.

What documents do you need for tax preparation?

Common items include W‑2s, 1099s, K‑1s, 1098s, brokerage tax statements, business income/expense summaries, depreciation schedules, and prior‑year returns. I’ll provide a tailored checklist during onboarding.

Do you work with remote clients?

Yes. I work with local and remote clients using secure document portals, e‑signature, and video calls. In‑person meetings are available by appointment in Fulton, IL.
